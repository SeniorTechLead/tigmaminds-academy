## Hindu Mythology (5 stories)

### 1. Why Hanuman Lifted a Mountain
**Story**: Hanuman flies to the Himalayas to bring the Sanjeevani herb to save Lakshmana. Unable to identify the right plant, he lifts the entire mountain and carries it across the sky.
**STEM**: Geology & Botany
- Level 0: How mountains form (tectonic plates), why rare plants grow only at certain altitudes (altitude zones, thin air, UV), medicinal plants and how we identify them
- Level 1: Altitude and air pressure, plant identification keys (dichotomous keys)
- Level 2: GPS coordinates and elevation mapping, botanical classification
- Level 3: Pharmacology — how plant compounds become medicine, extraction methods
- Level 4: Build a plant identification app using image classification

**Card prompt**: `Editorial watercolor illustration, a powerful monkey warrior soaring through a starlit sky carrying a glowing mountain peak on one palm, Himalayan herbs glowing golden on the mountain surface, dramatic clouds below, warm gold and indigo tones, no text --ar 16:9`

**Video prompt**: `Hanuman flying through clouds at night carrying a glowing mountain, herbs shimmering with golden light, clouds parting as he soars past, camera tracking alongside, cinematic, 5 seconds`

### 2. The Churning of the Ocean
**Story**: Devas and Asuras churn the cosmic ocean (Samudra Manthan) using Mount Mandara as the churning rod and Vasuki the serpent as the rope. From the ocean emerge treasures: Amrita (nectar), Lakshmi, poison, the moon.
**STEM**: Chemistry & Fluid Dynamics
- Level 0: What happens when you churn liquids (emulsions, separation), why oil and water don't mix, density layers
- Level 1: Centrifugal force, how cream separators work, density calculations
- Level 2: Chemical reactions — acids and bases, pH, why some reactions produce "poison" (toxic gases) and some produce "nectar" (useful compounds)
- Level 3: Distillation and fractional separation, petroleum refining as modern "ocean churning"
- Level 4: Build a density column experiment + simulate separation with code

**Card prompt**: `Editorial watercolor illustration, cosmic ocean being churned with a massive mountain as churning rod wrapped by a giant serpent, layers of colorful liquids separating — gold rising and dark sinking, gods and demons pulling on either side, rich jewel tones, no text --ar 16:9`

**Video prompt**: `Cosmic ocean churning, mountain rotating slowly as serpent coils around it, colorful liquid layers separating — gold nectar rising to surface, dark poison sinking, gods and demons pulling rhythmically, slow majestic camera push in, 5 seconds`

- Level 1: Neurons and synapses, reaction time experiments, single-core vs multi-core processors
- Level 2: CPU architecture — how computers do parallel processing, threads and cores
- Level 3: Neural networks — how AI mimics brain structure, parallel layers
- Level 4: Build a multi-threaded task scheduler simulation

**Card prompt**: `Editorial watercolor illustration, a majestic ten-headed king seated on a golden throne, each head contemplating a different ancient text, golden palace of Lanka in background, rich golds and deep purples, no text --ar 16:9`

**Story**: The Pushpaka Vimana was a flying chariot described in the Ramayana — it could fly anywhere the pilot wished, expand to carry any number of passengers, and moved as fast as thought.
**STEM**: Aerodynamics & Flight

- Level 1: Bernoulli's principle, wing shapes, paper airplane experiments
- Level 2: How jet engines work, thrust-to-weight ratio, helicopter vs airplane
- Level 3: Rocket propulsion, escape velocity, orbital mechanics
- Level 4: Build a flight simulator — model lift and drag forces on different wing shapes

### 5. Agni — The Science of Fire
**Story**: Agni, the fire god, is present in every flame — from the cooking hearth to the funeral pyre to the stars. The Vedas describe fire as the messenger between humans and gods, carrying offerings upward as smoke.

- Level 1: Chemical reactions in combustion, energy release, calories and joules
- Level 2: Heat transfer — conduction, convection, radiation. Why you feel warmth from a fire across the room
- Level 3: Spectroscopy — identifying elements by flame color (flame tests), how astronomers use this to know what stars are made of
- Level 4: Build a flame color simulator + spectral analysis tool

## Buddhist Mythology (5 stories)

### 6. The Bodhi Tree That Never Dies
**Story**: Siddhartha sat beneath a pipal (Bodhi) tree for 49 days until he attained enlightenment. The original Bodhi tree in Bodh Gaya is said to be a descendant of that tree, over 2,500 years old. Cuttings were carried to Sri Lanka, and from there back to India when the original died.
**STEM**: Plant Biology & Cloning
- Level 0: How trees reproduce — seeds vs cuttings (vegetative propagation), why a cutting is genetically identical to the parent (a natural clone), how old can trees get?
- Level 1: DNA, genes, and what "genetically identical" means, plant hormones (auxin) and root growth
- Level 2: Grafting techniques, how farmers clone fruit trees, tissue culture
- Level 3: Genetic modification vs cloning, CRISPR basics, ethics of cloning
- Level 4: Build a plant growth simulation modeling auxin distribution

**Card prompt**: `Editorial watercolor illustration, an ancient sacred pipal tree with massive spreading roots and canopy, a serene meditating figure beneath it, golden light filtering through heart-shaped leaves, Buddhist monastery in background, warm greens and gold, no text --ar 16:9`

**Video prompt**: `Gentle breeze moving through the leaves of an ancient Bodhi tree, golden light dappling through the canopy onto a figure meditating below, leaves slowly falling, camera slowly pulling back to reveal the massive tree, 5 seconds`

### 7. The Wheel of Dharma
**Story**: When Buddha gave his first sermon at Sarnath, he "set the wheel of Dharma in motion." The eight-spoked wheel represents the Noble Eightfold Path. The wheel appears on the Indian flag (Ashoka Chakra) and on every Buddhist temple.
**STEM**: Rotational Physics & Engineering
- Level 0: Why wheels work — reducing friction, rolling vs sliding. Why round wheels beat square ones. What makes a wheel spin smoothly (axles, bearings).
- Level 1: Angular momentum, torque, gear ratios — how a small force can spin a big wheel
- Level 2: Gyroscopes and stability — why a spinning wheel doesn't fall over (bicycles, tops)
- Level 3: Flywheels as energy storage, moment of inertia calculations
- Level 4: Build a gear ratio calculator + simulate a flywheel energy storage system

**Card prompt**: `Editorial watercolor illustration, a magnificent golden eight-spoked Dharma wheel atop an ancient stone pillar, deer park of Sarnath with peaceful deer grazing, warm sunrise light, serene atmosphere, no text --ar 16:9`

**Video prompt**: `Golden Dharma wheel slowly spinning atop an Ashoka pillar, sunrise light catching the spokes, deer grazing peacefully in the park below, gentle morning mist, camera slowly rising to eye level with the wheel, 5 seconds`

- Level 2: Standing waves, harmonics, why bells have overtones. How musical instruments produce different notes.
- Level 3: Fourier analysis — breaking complex sounds into simple waves. How noise-canceling headphones work.
- Level 4: Build a bell tone analyzer — record a bell, visualize its frequency spectrum

## Implementation Notes

### Illustration + Video Production Workflow

**Step 1: Card image (Midjourney)**
1. Paste the **Card prompt** into Midjourney
2. Generate 4 variants
3. Pick the best variant (upscale it)
4. Download as PNG, convert to WebP
5. Place in `content/illustrations/{slug}.webp`

**Step 2: Hero video (Runway Gen-3 or Kling)**
1. Upload the chosen Midjourney image as the **starting frame**
2. Paste the **Video prompt** as the motion description
3. Settings: 5 seconds, 24fps, no audio, loop-friendly
4. Download as MP4
5. Convert to WebM (smaller): `ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -c:v libvpx-vp2 -b:v 1M -an output.webm`
6. Place in `content/videos/{slug}.webm`

**Step 3: Wire into the lesson**
- `illustration` field → path to the WebP card image
- Add a `heroVideo` field → path to the WebM video (new field, needs LessonPage update)
- Card image shows on home page, lessons index, and as video fallback
- Video plays on lesson page hero section (autoplay, muted, loop)

**Tool recommendations:**

*Option A: Two-tool pipeline (Midjourney + Runway)*
- **Midjourney** for static card image → **Runway Gen-4 Turbo** for video (image-to-video)
- Best if Midjourney consistently produces better stills

*Option B: Single-tool pipeline (Runway only)*
- **Runway Gen-4 Turbo** can produce both stills AND video from the same prompt
- Tested with Hanuman — Runway's "Nano Banana" output matched/beat Midjourney's watercolor
- Simpler workflow, one tool, consistent style between still and video
- Needs more testing across other stories before committing

*Other video tools (if Runway isn't available):*
- **Kling 1.5** — good for slow subtle motion (leaves, water, fire)
- **Pika** — cheapest, fine for simple motion (flickering flames, flowing water)
- Avoid Sora for now — waitlist/limited access

**Quality checklist before publishing:**
- [ ] Video loops seamlessly (no visible cut at loop point)
- [ ] No text/watermark from the tool in the video
- [ ] Motion is subtle and reverent — no jarring movements
- [ ] File size under 2MB for WebM (compress if needed)
- [ ] Fallback: card image displays if video fails to load

### Sensitivity guidelines
- Never frame the science as "debunking" the story
- Use language like "the story imagines..." or "the real-world science behind this is..."
- Respect sacred elements — don't make the religious figures into cartoon characters
- Illustrations should be reverent in tone, not satirical
- Have someone from each tradition review before publishing
